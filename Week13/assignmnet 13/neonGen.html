About Neon Genesis Evangelion

Neon Genesis Evangelion is an anime television series produced by Gainax and Tatsunoko Production, directed by Hideaki Anno and broadcast on TV Tokyo from October 1995 to March 1996. The story is set in a future world where humanity is on the brink of extinction due to the emergence of monstrous beings known as Angels. The only hope for the survival of mankind is the Evangelion, a giant bio-machine that can only be piloted by specially selected children.

The series is famous for its complex themes, unique storytelling, and psychological character development. It has become a cult classic in the anime community and has had a significant influence on the medium.

Fun Fact
A fun fact about Neon Genesis Evangelion is that the series' creator, Hideaki Anno, struggled with depression during the production of the show and used the series as a way to explore his own psychological issues, which may have contributed to the show's complex and often dark themes.

Characters

Shinji Ikari

The primary protagonist of the series. He is a 14-year-old boy who is recruited by his father to pilot the Evangelion Unit-01.

Asuka Langley Soryu

A fiery, confident girl who pilots the Evangelion Unit-02.

Rei Ayanami

A quiet, reserved girl who pilots the Evangelion Unit-00.

Gendo Ikari

The commander of NERV and Shinji's father. He is secretive and manipulative.

Episodes

Episode 1: Angel Attack

In the year 2015, the world is on the brink of destruction. The only hope for mankind is the Evangelion, a giant humanoid machine that can only be piloted by children. When the Angels - mysterious giant beings - appear, mankind's battle for survival begins.

Episode 2: The Beast

Shinji continues his training as a pilot, but struggles with the overwhelming stress of the job. Meanwhile, a new Angel appears, and Asuka joins the battle.

Episode 3: A Transfer

A new transfer student arrives at Shinji's school, and it turns out that she's a pilot too. Asuka and Rei begin to compete with each other, while Shinji struggles with his own feelings of inadequacy.
